# Hospitality ERP Analytics Prototype

This repository is a SAP S/4HANA-inspired hospitality ERP analytics prototype built with deterministic synthetic data. It demonstrates how FI, CO, SD, MM, forecasting, SQL, Python automation, and BI-style reporting can support hotel management decision-making.

> **Scope honesty note:** This is not a real SAP S/4HANA implementation, does not connect to a live ERP system, and does not use confidential company data.

## Business scenario
A fictional multi-property hotel group needs a management reporting view that connects revenue, receivables, departmental costs, procurement, inventory, and short-term planning. The prototype shows how ERP-style datasets can be transformed into decision-ready KPIs, exception reports, Markdown analysis, SQL examples, SVG charts, and a static HTML dashboard.

## Business problem
Hospitality managers often review commercial performance, cash collection, purchasing, inventory, and cost control in separate reports. That separation makes it harder to answer practical questions such as: Are strong bookings converting to cash? Which cost centers are overspending? Are vendors creating service risk? Which inventory items require replenishment? Which forecast baseline is safe enough for planning discussion?

## Why ERP analytics matters in hospitality
Hotels operate through connected processes: order-to-cash, invoice-to-cash, procure-to-pay, inventory replenishment, and cost-center review. ERP analytics turns those process records into controls and management actions, helping leaders protect cash flow, profitability, guest service levels, and operational accountability.

## Module coverage

| FI | Customer invoices, payments, collection rate, open AR, aging buckets. |
| CO | Budget versus actual cost centers, variance %, operating profit, margin. |
| SD | Revenue category, channel, customer segment, occupancy, ADR, RevPAR. |
| MM | Vendors, purchase spend, delivery delay, reorder alerts, inventory risk. |

## Data model
Master data includes calendar, customers, and vendors. Transaction-style datasets include sales revenue, customer invoices, customer payments, cost center budget/actuals, procurement/vendor records, and inventory movements. The data is intentionally deterministic, text-based, and auditable.

## Key business findings demonstrated
- Revenue performance is linked to collection and open receivables, not treated as cash by default.
- Cost-center variance highlights departments requiring management review.
- Channel and customer segment reporting supports commercial strategy discussions.
- Vendor delay and reorder alerts show procurement and inventory risks before guest service is affected.
- Forecasting is intentionally simple and positioned as baseline planning support.

## Limitations
- Synthetic/anonymized data only.
- No live SAP connection, direct SAP table extraction, production deployment, or confidential data.
- Forecasting is not production-grade forecasting.
- No binary BI files are included; the dashboard is static HTML with text/SVG outputs.

## Interview positioning
Position this as an ERP analytics portfolio project: it proves you can connect business processes, accounting/ERP logic, Python automation, SQL-ready data, tests, and executive communication. Do not present it as an SAP implementation or live client project.
